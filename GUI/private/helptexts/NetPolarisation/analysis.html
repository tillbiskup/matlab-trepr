Analysis

The analysis panel provides you with a full overview of the net polarisation of your dataset.

Pretrigger statistics

This panel summarises statistical information gathered from the pretrigger part of your time traces, such as minimum, maximum, delta (max-min), average and standard deviation (std. dev.).

Net polarisation

The actually interesting part of the analysis is summed up in this subpanel. Here you find both, values and positions on the time axis for both minimum and maximum polarisation of your signal in the time range starting with your trigger signal.

Colour coding

To ease the analysis, the net polarisation fields are colour coded in three different colours: green, blue, and red. The meaning of these three colours is as follows:

green
No net polarisation
Criterion: Polarisation minima and maxima within the range of the pretrigger part of the signal.
blue
Net emissive polarisation.
Criterion: Value is smaller than the minimum of the pretrigger part of the signal.
red
Net absorptive polarisation.
Criterion: Value is bigger than the maximum of the pretrigger part of the signal.

To get a better impression of how large the deviation is, you can use the Display panel to display minima and maxima as coloured area.

1To calculate the deviation of the magnetic field, 2*stdev is used, as normally, +/- stdev is displayed as error margins.
